# Medicine After Death: XAI under Label Bias

[![License: MIT](https://img.shields.io/badge/License-MIT-yellow.svg)](https://opensource.org/licenses/MIT)
[![Status: Research Prototype](https://img.shields.io/badge/Status-Active%20Research-blue)](https://github.com/ojakintande/LLM_Who_Audit_Reviewers)

This repository contains the experimental implementation and analysis scripts for a study investigating how label bias propagates through machine learning pipelines to systematically distort the outputs of Explainable AI (XAI) methods. The core premise parallels a sobering medical analogy: applying corrective interventions to an already-compromised diagnostic foundation yields little more than a false sense of resolution. When bias infects the training data, the explanations generated by XAI methods become not merely incomplete, but fundamentally misleading - offering interpretability without truth.

---

## 1. Research Overview
Our study utilizes the **COMPAS recidivism dataset** to test the hypothesis that biased labeling frameworks directly compromise the reliability of model interpretability. We perform a comparative analysis between:
*   **Target Label (Less Biased):** The 2-year recidivism outcome.
*   **Proxy Label (Biased):** The COMPAS decile score.

The research demonstrates that when models are trained on biased proxies, XAI techniques often produce erroneous interpretations, leading to misleading insights about model behavior.

## 2. Methodology
*   **Dataset:** COMPAS Recidivism Dataset.
*   **Experimental Variable:** Evaluation of XAI interpretations derived from models trained on "less biased" vs. "biased" ground truth labels.
*   **Key Finding:** XAI methods applied to biased labels risk reinforcing incorrect conclusions, highlighting a critical failure point in current interpretability auditing practices.
